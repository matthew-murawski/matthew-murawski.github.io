Senior Thesis

"Independent Encoding of Salience, Value, and Attention in Primate Superior Colliculus"

  • Recorded 220 superior colliculus (SC) neurons in macaques performing a spatially cued covert change detection task and a saccade task that varied reward value and salience.
  • Investigated how SC activity is modulated by physical salience, reward, and attention cues.
  • Applied single-neuron ROC analyses and population-level support vector machine classification in MATLAB.
  • Findings complicate the unified priority map model, potentially suggesting that independent modulatory influences support context-specific visually guided behaviors.
